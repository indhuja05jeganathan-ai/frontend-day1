NELLAI

Tirunelveli is a historic city in southern Tamil Nadu, situated on the banks of the Thamirabarani River and popularly known as Nellai. It is one of the oldest cities, known for Nellaiappar Temple, Shaivite traditions, classical architecture, temple festivals, music, and folk arts

NELLAIAPPAR TEMPLE

The origins of Nellaiappar Temple are mentioned in the ancient Puranas and are devoted to Lord Shiva. The gopuram is attributed to Muluthukanda Rama Pandiyan, while the two shrines were connected in 1647 AD by Thiru Vadamalaiappa Pillaiyan through the Chain Mandapam.

Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve, the second largest protected forest in Tamil Nadu, spreads over 895 square kilometers in the Western Ghats at about 1,800 meters altitude. It is known for dense forests, rich biodiversity, endemic species, scenic waterfalls, and vital wildlife conservation.
